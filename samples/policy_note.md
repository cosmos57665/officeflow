# Office Leave & Reimbursement Policy

## Page 1: Purpose, Scope, and Leave Basics

BrightPath Office Services maintains this fictional policy so employees and managers
can handle common leave and reimbursement questions consistently. The policy applies
to full-time and part-time office staff after their first 30 calendar days of
employment. Contractors may use the reimbursement rules when a written statement of
work allows business expense repayment, but they do not receive paid leave.

Annual leave is provided for rest, personal travel, family events, and other planned
time away from work. Full-time employees receive 18 working days of annual leave each
calendar year. Part-time employees receive annual leave in proportion to their normal
weekly schedule. Unused annual leave may be carried forward up to 5 working days into
the next calendar year. Any annual leave above the 5-day carry-forward limit expires
on December 31 unless Human Resources approves an exception in writing.

Employees should request planned annual leave at least 10 working days before the
first day away. Requests are submitted to the direct manager by email or through the
office scheduling tool. Managers should respond within 3 working days and should
consider team coverage, client commitments, and fairness to other employees. Approved
leave is not considered final until it appears on the shared team calendar.

Sick leave is available when an employee is ill, recovering from treatment, attending
an urgent medical appointment, or caring for an immediate family member. Employees
should notify their manager as early as possible on the first day of absence. A
medical note may be requested when sick leave continues for more than 3 consecutive
working days.

## Page 2: Special Leave and Remote Work During Absence

Employees may request up to 5 paid bereavement leave days after the death of an
immediate family member. Immediate family includes a spouse or partner, child, parent,
sibling, grandparent, grandchild, or a person who lives in the employee's household.
Managers may approve additional unpaid leave when travel or ceremony dates require
more time.

Emergency personal leave may be used for unexpected events such as a home repair
crisis, school closure, legal appointment, or urgent family need. Emergency personal
leave is limited to 3 paid working days per calendar year. Employees should contact
their manager by phone or message before the workday begins whenever possible.

Public holidays announced by the company are paid non-working days. If a client
deadline requires an employee to work on a public holiday, the manager must approve
the work in advance. The employee may then take one replacement day within 60 calendar
days.

Remote work is not a substitute for approved leave. An employee who is too unwell to
work should use sick leave rather than working remotely. However, a manager may approve
remote work for part of a day when the employee can complete duties safely and the
arrangement does not disrupt meetings, customer service, or confidential information
handling.

## Page 3: Reimbursement Eligibility and Limits

Business expenses are reimbursed when they are necessary, reasonable, documented, and
approved by the correct manager. Eligible expenses include local travel to client
meetings, economy rail or air travel approved in advance, modest working meals during
approved travel, printing for client materials, and office supplies purchased for a
specific team need.

Employees must choose practical, cost-conscious options. Taxi or ride-share expenses
are reimbursed when public transportation is unavailable, unsafe, or would cause a
significant delay. Personal vehicle mileage is reimbursed at the monthly rate
published by Finance. Alcohol, entertainment, fines, personal clothing, personal
subscriptions, and upgrades to premium travel are not reimbursable.

Meal reimbursement during approved travel is capped at 800 INR for breakfast, 1,200
INR for lunch, and 1,500 INR for dinner. When a hotel or event provides a meal, the
employee should not claim a second meal for the same period. Tips may be reimbursed
only when they are customary and included on the receipt.

Receipts are required for every reimbursement claim above 500 INR. A receipt must show
the vendor, date, amount, and purchased item or service. If a receipt is lost, the
employee must submit a written explanation and manager approval. Finance may reject
claims that do not contain enough detail to confirm the business purpose.

## Page 4: Claim Submission, Approval, and Exceptions

Reimbursement claims must be submitted within 15 calendar days after the expense date.
Claims are submitted with receipts attached and a short business purpose for each
line item. Travel claims should include the client name, meeting date, and destination.
Late claims may be denied unless the employee explains the delay and Finance approves
the exception.

The direct manager approves ordinary expenses up to 10,000 INR. Department heads
approve claims above 10,000 INR or any claim involving overnight travel. Finance gives
final payment approval after checking receipts, policy limits, and budget coding.
Employees should not approve their own expenses or ask a peer to approve expenses on
their behalf.

Approved reimbursements are normally paid in the next payroll cycle. If a claim is
missing information, Finance will return it for correction within 5 working days.
Employees should keep original receipts until reimbursement appears in payroll.

Policy exceptions must be documented before the expense is incurred whenever possible.
Emergency exceptions may be reviewed afterward, but approval is not guaranteed. Human
Resources owns leave policy interpretation, Finance owns reimbursement interpretation,
and the Office Manager maintains the current published copy of this policy.
